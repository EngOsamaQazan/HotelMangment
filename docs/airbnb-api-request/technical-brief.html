0. Why a channel manager, not direct API?

Airbnb's own partner portal (https://www.airbnb.com/partner) states:

"At this time, we are not accepting new access requests for our API. Our global team of partner managers will reach out to prospective partners based on the supply opportunity your business represents, strength of your technology, and ability to support our shared customers."

Direct API access is closed to single properties. The only way to get real-time ARI and reservation sync with Airbnb is through an approved connectivity partner / channel manager. We are choosing SiteMinder, an officially approved Airbnb partner (see siteminder.com/channel-manager/airbnb-hotels).

1. Executive summary

We operate an in-house Property Management System (PMS) built specifically for our hotel group: 3 properties — property 14364167 (Al-Mafraq Hotel) already live on Booking.com, and two more being onboarded. We want the same property footprint to go live on Airbnb with the same guarantees: single source of truth in our PMS, real-time two-way sync, no double-bookings, and one operational dashboard for the whole channel mix.

Our PMS will integrate with SiteMinder's public pmsXchange and SiteMinder Exchange (SMX) APIs. SiteMinder then fans our inventory out to Airbnb, Booking.com, Expedia, Agoda, and 420+ other channels from one integration on our side.

The system is already in production at https://hotel.aqssat.co and is used daily by our staff.

The in-flight UnitType redesign (see docs/plans/unit-types-redesign.md) normalises exactly the fields SiteMinder expects: maxOccupancy, bedroomCount, livingRoomCount, bathroomCount, per-type bed list, amenity list, photo gallery, and seasonal Rate records.

Listing strategy: one Airbnb listing per UnitType, with inventory count equal to the number of physical units of that type. This matches Airbnb's "Hotels / Boutique Hotel" model and avoids the operational pain of 20+ identical per-room listings.

5. APIs we will consume from SiteMinder (priority order)

  1. pmsXchange · Content / Room setup — room types, amenities, bed configurations, photos, descriptions (OTA_HotelDescriptiveContentNotif, OTA_HotelRoomList).
  2. pmsXchange · ARI (Availability, Rates, Inventory) — nightly availability, nightly rates, restrictions (OTA_HotelAvailNotif, OTA_HotelRateAmountNotif, OTA_HotelInvCountNotif).
  3. SiteMinder Exchange (SMX) · Reservation retrieval — new, modified, and cancelled reservations from every connected channel, including Airbnb (REST/JSON with SOAP/XML fallback).
  4. SiteMinder webhooks / notifications — push reservations to our PMS within ~30s (vs. polling).
  5. Nightly content reconciliation — drift detection between PMS and SiteMinder content store.

Every stream will be idempotent (SiteMinder message IDs), retried with exponential backoff + dead-letter queue, and carry structured logs with correlation IDs across PMS ↔ SiteMinder ↔ OTA. Channel-level feature flags let us pause a single OTA (e.g. Airbnb) without affecting the others.

7. Security & compliance

  • TLS end-to-end (Let's Encrypt, auto-renewed).
  • AES-256-GCM at rest for all third-party credentials; no plaintext storage.
  • HMAC-verified inbound webhooks (already implemented for Booking; reused for SiteMinder).
  • Full audit log for reservation edits, rate changes, permission changes, channel mapping changes.
  • GDPR-aware: guest data can be anonymised / exported on request.
  • Edge rate limiting (src/lib/rateLimit.ts).
  • Ready to sign and honour a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with SiteMinder.
